# dict_math5 — Closed Dictionary (Math, 5-year-old level)

> **Rule**: Every word in every definition must itself be defined in this dictionary.
> Extension vocabulary for mathematical reasoning.
> Shares ground words with dict5 — these are bridge terms.

---

## GROUND WORDS (shared with dict5 — bridge terms)

**thing** — all that is.
- "a number is a thing"
- "a result is a thing"
- "all of this is a thing"

**is** — tells what a thing is.
- "one is a number"
- "two plus one is three"
- "the result is a number"

**a** — one of a thing.
- "a number"
- "a big number"
- "a result"

**not** — not yes is no. not good is bad. not big is small.
- "not equal"
- "not more"
- "three is not two"

**it** — the thing. a thing you see or name.
- "two plus one. it is three."
- "the result. what is it?"
- "a number. it can be big."

**and** — this with that.
- "one and two"
- "plus and minus"
- "three and four"

**the** — that one thing.
- "the number"
- "the result"
- "the answer"

**of** — a part of a thing. the color of the ball.
- "the result of two plus three"
- "part of a number"
- "the name of a number"

**can** — a thing can do a thing.
- "you can count"
- "you can add numbers"
- "a number can be big or small"

**what** — you see a thing. what is it? what is the name?
- "what is two plus three?"
- "what number is it?"
- "what is the result?"

**yes** — it is. not no.
- "is three a number? yes."
- "is two plus one three? yes."
- "is five more than two? yes."

**no** — it is not. not yes.
- "is two equal to three? no."
- "is one more than five? no."
- "is zero a big number? no."

**you** — the person that sees this and feels this. not the dog, not the cat. you.
- "you count to five"
- "you can add"
- "you see the result"

**big** — not small. the sun is big.
- "a big number"
- "five is big"
- "ten is a big number"

**small** — not big. the cat is small.
- "a small number"
- "one is small"
- "zero is the most small"

**to** — a move to a place. a give to a person.
- "count to five"
- "add one to two"
- "equal to three"

**with** — a thing and a thing, not one.
- "two with three is five"
- "count with your hand"
- "add with plus"

**has** — a thing has a thing in it or on it or with it.
- "a number has a name"
- "five has five ones in it"
- "the result has a number"

**one** — not many. one thing. one dog.
- "one is the first number"
- "one and one is two"
- "one thing"

**all** — not one, not part. all things. all of it.
- "all numbers"
- "count all of it"
- "all ones in five"

**make** — you do a thing and a not-thing is a thing. you make food. you make a sound.
- "two plus three makes five"
- "you make a result"
- "add makes a number big"

**do** — to make a thing. you do it.
- "what can you do with numbers?"
- "do the count"
- "you do plus"

**name** — a sound that is of one thing. "dog" is the name of an animal.
- "five is the name of a number"
- "plus is the name of an action"
- "the name of the result"

**in** — a thing is in a thing.
- "five has five ones in it"
- "the result is in the count"
- "two is in three"

**this** — the thing that is to you. not that.
- "this number is three"
- "this is the result"
- "count this"

**see** — you see a thing that is not on you. you see the dog. you see the sun.
- "you see the number"
- "see the result"
- "you can see how many"

**give** — you has a thing. you give it. you not has it. a person has it.
- "two plus three gives five"
- "counting gives a number"
- "the result gives the answer"

**part** — a thing that is of a thing. not all, one of a thing.
- "two is part of five"
- "one is a part of all numbers"
- "a part of the count"

## NUMBER WORDS

**number** — a thing that tells how many. one, two, three are numbers.
- "five is a number"
- "a big number"
- "what number is it?"

**zero** — the number of no things. not one. not any.
- "zero is a number"
- "zero is not one"
- "you have zero things"

**two** — the number after one. one and one is two.
- "two is a number"
- "one plus one is two"
- "two is more than one"

**three** — the number after two. two and one is three.
- "three is a number"
- "two plus one is three"
- "three is more than two"

**four** — the number after three. three and one is four.
- "four is a number"
- "three plus one is four"
- "four is more than three"

**five** — the number after four. four and one is five.
- "five is a number"
- "four plus one is five"
- "five is more than four"

**ten** — a big number. five and five is ten.
- "ten is a number"
- "five plus five is ten"
- "ten is more than five"

**many** — more than one. not one. a big number of things.
- "many numbers"
- "five is many"
- "many things"

## OPERATION WORDS

**plus** — put together. two plus three is five. plus makes more.
- "one plus one is two"
- "three plus two is five"
- "plus makes a number big"

**minus** — take one from the other. five minus two is three. minus makes less.
- "five minus one is four"
- "three minus two is one"
- "minus makes a number small"

**equal** — the same number. two plus three is equal to five. not more, not less.
- "three is equal to three"
- "one plus one is equal to two"
- "equal is the same"

**more** — a big number has more than a small number. five is more than two.
- "three is more than one"
- "five is more than four"
- "more is not less"

**less** — a small number has less than a big number. two is less than five.
- "one is less than three"
- "two is less than five"
- "less is not more"

**count** — say numbers in order. one, two, three is to count.
- "count to five"
- "count the things"
- "you count one, two, three"

**how** — to ask of a way. how many? how do you count?
- "how many is two plus three?"
- "how do you count?"
- "how is it equal?"

**result** — the number that you get. the result of two plus three is five.
- "the result is five"
- "what is the result?"
- "the result of counting"

**after** — the thing that comes next. two is after one. three is after two.
- "two is after one"
- "three is after two"
- "what comes after four?"

**first** — the one that is not after any. one is the first number.
- "one is the first number"
- "the first thing"
- "count the first one"

**same** — not a different thing. equal is the same.
- "two plus one is the same as three"
- "the same number"
- "same is equal"

**order** — things that come after things. one, two, three is an order.
- "numbers have an order"
- "count in order"
- "the order is one, two, three"

**any** — one of all. not this one, not that one, any one.
- "any number"
- "is it any of the numbers?"
- "any thing can have a number"
