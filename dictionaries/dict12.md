# dict12 — Closed Dictionary (12-year-old level)

> **Rule**: Every word in every definition must itself be defined in this dictionary.
> Basic inflections (-s, -ed, -ing, -es, -er, -est, -ly) are forms of the base word.
> Core entries: same 50 words as dict5, with richer definitions.
> Additional entries: all words needed to close the dictionary.
> Each entry: definition + 3 usage examples.

---

## CORE WORDS (50) — Upgraded Definitions

### Ground Words

**thing** — anything that exists or can be thought about. objects, ideas, and events are all things.
- "every object is a thing"
- "a thought is also a thing"
- "name the things you can see"

**is** — connects a thing to what it is, how it is, or where it is. used to describe or identify.
- "the earth is round"
- "water is a liquid"
- "the sun is bright"

**a** — one of something. any single member of a group.
- "a person walked by"
- "give me a reason"
- "a dog is a type of animal"

**not** — the opposite or absence of something. turns a true statement false.
- "ice is not hot"
- "a rock is not alive"
- "she is not here"

**it** — refers to a thing already mentioned or known. takes the place of a name.
- "it is cold outside."
- "take the ball and throw it."
- "it is hard to say"

### Structural Words

**and** — joins two or more things, ideas, or actions together.
- "smart and kind"
- "water and food"
- "she is strong and fast"

**or** — presents a choice between two or more options. one or the other.
- "yes or no"
- "hot or cold"
- "you can walk or run"

**the** — points to a specific thing already known or mentioned.
- "the sun is bright"
- "pass the ball"
- "the reason is clear"

**to** — shows direction, purpose, or connection between actions.
- "walk to the door"
- "she wants to learn"
- "give it to me"

**of** — shows belonging, connection, or what something is made of or part of.
- "the color of the sky"
- "a part of the body"
- "the sound of water"

**in** — inside something. within a space, time, or group.
- "she arrived in the morning"
- "the best player in the group"
- "the animal is in the water"

**on** — touching the surface of something. about a subject.
- "the light is on"
- "the thing is on the table"
- "a talk on the subject"

**has** — owns, contains, or includes something. shows possession or features.
- "the dog has soft fur"
- "she has three brothers"
- "this room has two windows"

**can** — is able to do something. has the skill, power, or permission.
- "she can speak two languages"
- "you can try again"
- "she can run fast"

**with** — together with. using something. having a feature.
- "water with salt"
- "cut it with a tool"
- "a person with strength"

**this** — points to something near or just mentioned. the specific thing here.
- "look at this"
- "this is the right answer"
- "this is a good idea"

### Question / Response Words

**what** — asks about the identity, nature, or type of something.
- "what is your name?"
- "what happened?"
- "what type of animal is that?"

**yes** — confirms that something is true or correct. agreement.
- "yes, that is right"
- "did you see it? yes."
- "yes, I understand"

**no** — denies that something is true. refusal or absence.
- "no, that is wrong"
- "there is no water left"
- "she said no"

**you** — the person being spoken to.
- "what do you think?"
- "I will help you"
- "you can do it"

### Property Words

**big** — large in size, amount, or importance. more than normal.
- "a big place"
- "a big change"
- "the animal is big"

**small** — little in size, amount, or importance. less than normal.
- "a small amount of salt"
- "the change was small but important"
- "a small animal"

**good** — having positive qualities. useful, kind, or well done. not harmful.
- "a good person helps others"
- "the food tastes good"
- "she did a good job"

**bad** — having negative qualities. harmful, wrong, or poor in quality.
- "a bad decision"
- "bad weather is a problem"
- "it is bad to lie"

**hot** — having a high temperature. much warmer than the body.
- "the sun is hot"
- "hot water"
- "the land is hot and dry"

**cold** — having a low temperature. less warm than the body.
- "the ice is cold"
- "a cold glass of water"
- "it is cold outside"

**up** — toward a higher position or level. the opposite direction of down.
- "climb up the hill"
- "look up at the sky"
- "she picked it up"

**down** — toward a lower position or level. the opposite direction of up.
- "water flows down"
- "sit down on the ground"
- "the temperature goes down"

### Action Words

**see** — to notice or observe something using your eyes. to understand.
- "I can see the mountain from here"
- "do you see what I mean?"
- "she can see it"

**feel** — to experience a sensation or emotion through the body or mind.
- "I feel the warmth of the sun"
- "she feels happy"
- "feel the surface of this material"

**move** — to change position or location. to go from one place to another.
- "the clouds move across the sky"
- "she moved to the door"
- "the dog moved fast"

**make** — to create, build, or cause something to happen.
- "they made a decision"
- "she can make food"
- "loud sounds make me want to leave"

**eat** — to take food into the body through the mouth for energy.
- "people eat plants and food from animals"
- "the cat eats two times a day"
- "we eat together"

**give** — to transfer something from yourself to someone else. to provide.
- "give me your opinion"
- "trees give us oxygen"
- "she gives him the ball"

**live** — to be alive. to exist in a place over time. to experience life.
- "we live and learn"
- "animals live in water"
- "she lives in a small place"

**do** — to perform an action or task. to carry out.
- "what do you want to do?"
- "she did her best"
- "do the right thing"

### Category Words

**animal** — a living thing that can move on its own, sense its surroundings, and needs food for energy. not a plant.
- "a large animal"
- "animals need food and water"
- "some animals are wild"

**person** — a human being. an individual with thoughts, the ability to feel, and the ability to communicate and reason.
- "every person has rights"
- "she is a kind person"
- "a person can learn at any age"

**food** — any substance that a living thing eats to get energy and nutrients for growth and survival.
- "animals search for food daily"
- "the taste of food can change"
- "food gives us energy"

**water** — a clear liquid with no color or taste that all living things need to survive. it falls as rain and fills rivers and oceans.
- "drink enough water every day"
- "water freezes into ice below zero"
- "plants need water and light to grow"

**color** — the quality of light that the eye sees, such as red, blue, or green. objects reflect light differently to show different colors.
- "mix blue and yellow to get green"
- "the color of the ground is dark"
- "color can change how you feel"

**place** — a specific area, location, or position where something exists or happens.
- "this is a quiet place to study"
- "every object has a place"
- "a safe place"

**sound** — a vibration that travels through air or other material and can be heard by the ear.
- "sound moves faster through water than air"
- "the sound is loud"
- "sound is a type of energy"

**part** — a piece or section of something larger. not the whole, but a portion.
- "the brain is part of the body"
- "she played a part in the play"
- "this is only part of the whole"

**name** — a word or group of words that identifies a specific person, place, thing, or idea. a label.
- "what is the name of this plant?"
- "she changed her last name"
- "she has a good name"

**one** — a single unit. the number between zero and two. also means a certain thing.
- "give me one reason"
- "one of the best ideas"
- "one person can make a change"

**all** — every single one. the whole amount or group. nothing left out.
- "all living things need water"
- "all people must help"
- "she used all the food"

### Entity Words

**dog** — a domestic mammal closely related to the wolf. dogs have been companions to humans for thousands of years. they can be trained to follow commands, guard, and assist people.
- "dogs have a strong sense of smell"
- "a loyal dog protects its family"
- "the dog is fast"

**cat** — a small domestic mammal with soft fur, sharp claws, and keen night vision. cats are independent hunters that have lived with humans for thousands of years.
- "the cat climbed the tree"
- "cats sleep for many hours each day"
- "a cat can see well in low light"

**sun** — the star at the center of our solar system. it provides light and heat that make life on earth possible. it is extremely large and hot.
- "plants use energy from the sun to grow"
- "the sun is a star"
- "the sun is very large and hot"

**ball** — a round object used in many sports and games. it can bounce, roll, or be moved through the air.
- "the ball bounced off the wall"
- "the ball is round"
- "a small ball is light"
