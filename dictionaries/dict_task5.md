# dict_task5 — Closed Dictionary (Task Dispatcher, 5-year-old level)

> **Rule**: Every word in every definition must itself be defined in this dictionary.
> Minimal vocabulary for routing queries to the correct domain.
> Bridge terms connect to dict5, dict_math5, and dict_grammar5.

## GROUND WORDS (shared with dict5)

**thing** — all that is.
- "a question is a thing"
- "an answer is a thing"
- "a task is a thing"

**is** — tells what a thing is.
- "what is this?"
- "this is a question"
- "the answer is the result"

**a** — one of a thing.
- "a question"
- "a task"
- "an answer"

**not** — not yes is no. not good is bad. not big is small.
- "not a question"
- "not the same task"
- "this is not that"

**it** — the thing. a thing you see or name.
- "a question. answer it."
- "the task. do it."
- "the result. see it."

**and** — this with that.
- "count and write"
- "question and answer"
- "words and numbers"

**the** — that one thing.
- "the question"
- "the answer"
- "the result"

**what** — you see a thing. what is it? what is the name?
- "what is the question?"
- "what do you want?"
- "what is the answer?"

**can** — a thing can do a thing.
- "you can ask"
- "you can count"
- "you can write"

**you** — the person that sees this and feels this.
- "you ask a question"
- "you want an answer"
- "you tell a task"

**to** — a move to a place. a give to a person.
- "to ask a question"
- "to give an answer"
- "to do a task"

**do** — to make a thing. you do it.
- "do the task"
- "what do you do?"
- "do the count"

**make** — you do a thing and a not-thing is a thing.
- "make an answer"
- "make a sentence"
- "make a result"

**yes** — it is. not no.
- "is this a question? yes."
- "can you count? yes."
- "is the answer good? yes."

**no** — it is not. not yes.
- "is this an answer? no."
- "no, this is not a number task."
- "no, this is not a word task."

**has** — a thing has a thing in it or on it or with it.
- "a question has words"
- "a task has an answer"
- "a sentence has meaning"

**with** — a thing and a thing, not one.
- "answer with words"
- "answer with a number"
- "a task with numbers"

**in** — a thing is in a thing.
- "a number is in the question"
- "a word is in the question"
- "the answer is in the result"

**this** — the thing that is to you. not that.
- "this is a question"
- "this is a number task"
- "this is a word task"

**see** — you see a thing that is not on you.
- "see the question"
- "see what kind of task"
- "you see the answer"

**give** — you has a thing. you give it. you not has it. a person has it.
- "give the answer"
- "give a result"
- "give the sentence"

**good** — not bad. you feel good.
- "a good answer"
- "a good sentence"
- "a good result"

**bad** — not good.
- "a bad answer"
- "a bad sentence"
- "a bad result"

**name** — a sound that is of one thing.
- "the name of the task"
- "number is a name for a kind"
- "word is a name for a kind"

**all** — not one, not part. all things. all of it.
- "all tasks"
- "use all words"
- "count all numbers"

## TASK WORDS

**question** — words that ask a thing. "what is two plus three?" is a question. a question wants an answer.
- "this is a question"
- "a question has words"
- "answer the question"

**answer** — what you give to a question. the answer tells the result.
- "the answer is five"
- "give the answer"
- "the answer is a sentence"

**ask** — to make a question. you ask, and you want an answer.
- "you ask a question"
- "ask with words"
- "ask what the result is"

**want** — you want a thing. you do not have it. you ask for it.
- "you want an answer"
- "the question wants a number"
- "the question wants words"

**task** — a thing to do. a question is a task. count is a task. write is a task.
- "this is a task"
- "do the task"
- "what kind of task?"

**kind** — one of many. a number task is a kind of task. a word task is a kind of task.
- "what kind is this?"
- "a kind of question"
- "a kind of answer"

## DOMAIN BRIDGE WORDS

**number** — a thing that tells how many. one, two, three are numbers. number tasks use count and plus and minus.
- "a number is a thing"
- "the answer is a number"
- "a number task"

**word** — a thing you say or see that has meaning. word tasks use sentences and nouns and verbs.
- "a word is a thing"
- "the answer has words"
- "a word task"

**count** — say numbers in order. count is a number task.
- "count to five"
- "count is a number task"
- "you count numbers"

**write** — make words in order. write is a word task. make a sentence.
- "write a sentence"
- "write the answer"
- "write is a word task"

**sentence** — words in order that tell a thing. a sentence is the answer to a word task.
- "the answer is a sentence"
- "write a sentence"
- "a sentence has words"

**result** — the number that you get. a result is the answer to a number task.
- "the result is five"
- "the result is a number"
- "give the result"

**how** — to ask of a way. how many? how do you say it?
- "how many is it?"
- "how do you write this?"
- "how do you count?"

**many** — more than one. not one.
- "how many?"
- "many words"
- "many numbers"

**plus** — put together. two plus three is five. plus is a number task.
- "two plus three"
- "plus is a number task"
- "use plus to count"

**minus** — take one from the other. minus is a number task.
- "five minus two is three"
- "minus is a number task"
- "use minus to count"

## CONTENT BRIDGE WORDS

**content** — a kind of task. not a number task. not a word task. a content task asks what a thing is.
- "what is a dog is a content task"
- "is a thing hot is a content task"
- "a content task is not a number task"

**animal** — a thing with a name. it is not a number. it is not a word. animal is a content thing.
- "is a dog an animal is a content task"
- "animal is a content name"
- "animal is not a number"

**hot** — not cold. hot is not a number. hot is not a word. ask what is hot is a content task.
- "is the thing hot is a content task"
- "hot is a content thing"
- "hot is not a number"

**cold** — not hot. cold is not a number. cold is not a word. ask what is cold is a content task.
- "is the thing cold is a content task"
- "cold is a content thing"
- "cold is not a number"

## SELF BRIDGE WORDS

**self** — a kind of task. a self task asks what dafhne is. a self task asks what dafhne can do. a self task is not a number task. a self task is not a word task. a self task is not a content task.
- "what are you is a self task"
- "can you count is a self task"
- "a self task is not a number task"

**dafhne** — a thing that can read and learn. it is not a person. dafhne does self tasks.
- "dafhne can read"
- "dafhne can count"
- "what is dafhne is a self task"

**learn** — to see a thing and know it.
- "dafhne can learn"
- "learn is a self thing"
- "to learn is not to count"

**read** — to see words and know the words. dafhne can read.
- "dafhne can read"
- "read is a self thing"
- "to read is not to count"
