## GROUND WORDS (shared with dict5 — bridge terms)

**thing** — all that is.
- "a word is a thing"
- "a sentence is a thing"
- "all that you say is a thing"

**is** — tells what a thing is.
- "dog is a noun"
- "eat is a verb"
- "this is a sentence"

**a** — one of a thing.
- "a word"
- "a sentence"
- "a noun"

**not** — not yes is no. not good is bad. not big is small.
- "a verb is not a noun"
- "not a sentence"
- "not the same word"

**it** — the thing. a thing you see or name.
- "a word. it is a noun."
- "a sentence. it has words."
- "the subject. it does the action."

**and** — this with that.
- "a noun and a verb"
- "words and sentences"
- "subject and action"

**the** — that one thing.
- "the word"
- "the sentence"
- "the subject of the sentence"

**of** — a part of a thing. the color of the ball.
- "the subject of a sentence"
- "the meaning of a word"
- "part of a sentence"

**can** — a thing can do a thing.
- "a noun can be a subject"
- "a verb can tell an action"
- "you can make a sentence"

**what** — you see a thing. what is it? what is the name?
- "what is a noun?"
- "what does this word mean?"
- "what is the subject?"

**yes** — it is. not no.
- "is dog a noun? yes."
- "is eat a verb? yes."
- "is this a sentence? yes."

**no** — it is not. not yes.
- "is dog a verb? no."
- "is big a noun? no."
- "is this not a word? no."

**you** — the person that sees this and feels this. not the dog, not the cat. you.
- "you say words"
- "you make sentences"
- "you can tell things"

**to** — a move to a place. a give to a person.
- "a word to tell a thing"
- "to say a sentence"
- "to tell what a thing does"

**with** — a thing and a thing, not one.
- "a noun with a verb"
- "a sentence with many words"
- "say it with words"

**has** — a thing has a thing in it or on it or with it.
- "a sentence has words"
- "a word has a meaning"
- "a sentence has a subject"

**one** — not many. one thing. one dog.
- "one word"
- "one sentence"
- "one meaning"

**all** — not one, not part. all things. all of it.
- "all words"
- "all sentences have a subject"
- "all nouns are words"

**make** — you do a thing and a not-thing is a thing. you make food. you make a sound.
- "you make a sentence with words"
- "make a noun and a verb"
- "words make meaning"

**do** — to make a thing. you do it.
- "what does a verb do?"
- "a verb tells what you do"
- "do the action"

**name** — a sound that is of one thing. "dog" is the name of an animal.
- "a noun is a name for a thing"
- "verb is the name for an action word"
- "the name of a word"

**in** — a thing is in a thing.
- "a word is in a sentence"
- "the noun is in the sentence"
- "meaning is in a word"

**this** — the thing that is to you. not that.
- "this word is a noun"
- "this is a sentence"
- "this tells the meaning"

**see** — you see a thing that is not on you. you see the dog. you see the sun.
- "you see a word"
- "see the sentence"
- "you can see what it means"

**give** — you has a thing. you give it. you not has it. a person has it.
- "a noun gives a name to a thing"
- "a verb gives an action to a sentence"
- "a word gives meaning"

**part** — a thing that is of a thing. not all, one of a thing.
- "a word is a part of a sentence"
- "the subject is a part of a sentence"
- "a noun is part of a sentence"

**on** — a thing is on a thing.
- "the meaning is on the word"
- "a property is on a thing"
- "it is on the sentence"

**person** — an animal that can make things and give names.
- "a person says words"
- "a person makes sentences"
- "a person can tell things"

**animal** — a thing that lives. it can move. it can eat. it can feel.
- "dog is an animal"
- "animal is a noun"
- "an animal is a thing"

**good** — not bad. you feel good.
- "a good sentence has a subject and a verb"
- "a good word has meaning"
- "good order in a sentence"

**bad** — not good. you feel bad.
- "a bad sentence has no verb"
- "bad order in words"
- "bad is not good"

## GRAMMAR WORDS

**word** — a thing you say or see that has meaning. dog is a word. big is a word.
- "a word is a part of a sentence"
- "you say words"
- "all words have meaning"

**sentence** — words in order that tell a thing. "the dog eats" is a sentence. a sentence has a subject and a verb.
- "you make a sentence with words"
- "a sentence has meaning"
- "a good sentence tells a thing"

**noun** — a word that is a name for a thing. dog is a noun. sun is a noun. person is a noun.
- "dog is a noun"
- "a noun can be a subject"
- "a noun tells what a thing is"

**verb** — a word that tells what a thing does. eat is a verb. move is a verb. see is a verb.
- "eat is a verb"
- "a verb tells an action"
- "a sentence has a verb"

**subject** — the thing in a sentence that does the action. in "the dog eats", dog is the subject.
- "the subject does the action"
- "a sentence has a subject"
- "a noun can be a subject"

**action** — what a thing does. eat is an action. move is an action. a verb tells an action.
- "the subject does an action"
- "a verb tells what action"
- "eat is an action"

**property** — a word that tells how a thing is. big is a property. hot is a property. good is a property.
- "big is a property"
- "a property tells how"
- "a thing can have a property"

**meaning** — what a word tells. the meaning of dog is an animal. the meaning of big is not small.
- "a word has a meaning"
- "the meaning of a noun is a thing"
- "meaning is what you see in a word"

**order** — things that come after things. in a sentence, the subject comes first.
- "words have an order in a sentence"
- "the subject is first in the order"
- "order tells how to say it"

**tell** — to say a thing to a person. you tell with words. a word tells a meaning.
- "a noun tells a name"
- "a verb tells an action"
- "you tell with a sentence"

**say** — to make words. you say a sentence. you say words.
- "say the word"
- "you say a sentence"
- "say it in order"

**how** — to ask of a way. how is it? how do you say it?
- "how do you say this?"
- "a property tells how a thing is"
- "how do you make a sentence?"

**first** — the one that is not after any. the subject is first in a sentence.
- "the subject is first"
- "the first word"
- "first the noun, then the verb"

**after** — the thing that comes next. the verb comes after the subject.
- "the verb is after the subject"
- "after the noun comes the verb"
- "what comes after?"

**many** — more than one. not one. a big number of things.
- "many words"
- "a sentence has many words"
- "many nouns in a sentence"

**means** — what a word is. dog means an animal. big means not small.
- "dog means an animal"
- "verb means an action word"
- "what does this word means?"
